The Crack Shack Internship

Description

Intro

Spring Quarter 2020, I was a Design Intern through the Academic Internship Program (AIP) at UC San Diego. It was my second quarter working for the Crack Shack, a gourmet chicken restaurant based in Little Italy in Downtown San Diego. During the COVID-19 Global Pandemic, I had no choice but to work remotely under the guidance of my supervisor, Nicole Rogers, and was able to make the best out of the experience despite the circumstance.

For this final project submission, I wanted to practice my HTML and CSS skills, so I coded this webpage as an extension of my incomplete portfolio.

Tackling the COVID-19 Crisis

I took on this internship as a 2 unit course, meaning I cut my hours down to half of last quarter's. I was able to accomplish two of my learning objectives, whereas one had to be put aside to focus on more pressing issues. Though the internship was during a global pandemic and we had to change gears, I still got the experience adapting to changes beyond the control of a company.

Signs

As restrictions started lifting, the Crack Shack restaurants had to plan for reopening. Though restrictions were lifting, there would still need to be social distancing and compliance with the CDC guidelines. To tackle that issue, signs with QR codes were implemented.

Hop In Line

This first sign was meant for those waiting in line to order food. Equipped with a QR code, the sign was able to link customers to a Toast online ordering webpage so that they could get out of line and leave room for others.

Curbside Pickup

The Curbside Pickup signs were meant for customers who did not wat to get out of their car. They were able to scan a QR code for to order online from their parking spot, and they would get their food brought out to their table
